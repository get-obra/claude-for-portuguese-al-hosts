# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We, the maintainers and contributors of **Claude for Portuguese AL hosts**, commit to making participation in this project a respectful and constructive experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, experience level, or technical expertise.

## Standards we expect

Behavior that contributes to a positive environment includes:

- Treating other contributors with respect and good faith.
- Welcoming questions from people who are new to the domain, the codebase, or open source.
- Giving and accepting technical feedback graciously.
- Acknowledging that disagreement on technical and compliance choices is normal and healthy.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and the operators it serves.

## Behavior we will not accept

Harassment or disrespectful conduct of any form has no place in this project. This includes personal attacks, sustained disruption of discussion, disclosure of others' private information, or any conduct that a reasonable participant would find threatening or hostile.

For the canonical definitions and examples of unacceptable conduct, we adopt the standards set out in the [Contributor Covenant version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/). Where this document is silent or ambiguous, the Contributor Covenant governs.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions, mailing lists, chat channels, and any in-person or online events held under the project's name. It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public.

## Enforcement

Reports of conduct that may violate this Code can be sent to **`team@get-obra.com`**. All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly. The maintainers will respect the privacy and security of the reporter to the extent possible.

Maintainers who receive a report will:

1. Acknowledge receipt within seventy-two hours.
2. Review the report and any context with the people involved.
3. Decide on an appropriate response, which may include a private conversation, a public clarification, a temporary suspension from project spaces, or permanent removal from the project.
4. Communicate the decision to the reporter and, where appropriate, to the wider community.

Maintainers who do not follow this Code in good faith may face the same consequences as any other contributor.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is informed by the [Contributor Covenant version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/), to which we defer for canonical definitions. The full Contributor Covenant text is incorporated by reference.

## Questions

For anything that is not a conduct report — clarifications, suggestions, additions — feel free to open an issue or reach out at `team@get-obra.com`.
